A visual argument of:

"Here’s my AI policy for students: I don’t have one"

- Jonathan Zimmerman

This image puts the argument made by the author of this article in a multimodal form to grab the reader’s attention and clarify itself for the reader, showcasing the relationship between the student and the AI in his screen.

The AI reaching through the screen and onto the keyboard showcases the lack of identity and thought in the student, instead relying on the AI for his work.

The student's dreary look implies a sense of disconnection from his work, as well as an underlying fear or unease of the AI's control.

This is an interactive image. Try clicking on certain parts!

Overall, this message was successful in aiding the author’s argument and conveying the warning about AI’s potential overreach, taking control of a student’s work and all their independent and critical thinking skills.

Ethos:
The image establishes credibility through common concerns about AI in the education industry.

Pathos:
It invokes pathos through a sense of fear of losing control, framing the argument in an image of a robot literally taking control of the person. The dark and gloomy feel emphasizes guilt.

Kairos:
It is timely with the current situation around AI.
